IRON MAN
Iron Man is the superhero persona of Anthony Edward "Tony" Stark, a businessman and engineer who runs the weapons manufacturing company Stark Industries. When Stark was captured in a war zone and sustained a severe heart wound, he built his Iron Man armor and escaped his captors. Iron Man's suits of armor grant him superhuman strength, flight, energy projection, and other abilities.

BLACK WIDOW
Black Widow, known as Natasha Romanoff, is a highly skilled spy, assassin, and former KGB agent. Trained in the infamous Red Room program, she is an expert in martial arts, marksmanship, and espionage, making her one of the deadliest operatives in the world. Despite her dark past, Natasha seeks redemption by using her talents for good, eventually becoming a key member of the Avengers. With her sharp intellect, unwavering determination, and unbreakable loyalty to her teammates, Black Widow is a formidable hero who proves that strength comes in many forms.

CAPTAIN AMERICA
Captain America, also known as Steve Rogers, is the embodiment of bravery, honor, and sacrifice in the Marvel Universe. Created during World War II, Rogers was transformed from a frail young man into a super-soldier with peak human abilities through the Super-Soldier Serum. Armed with his iconic vibranium shield, Captain America became a symbol of hope and resilience, leading the fight against tyranny. As a founding member of the Avengers, he stands as a moral compass and a natural leader, always striving to protect the innocent and uphold justice, even when faced with overwhelming odds.

THOR
Thor, the Norse god of thunder, is one of the most powerful and iconic superheroes in the Marvel Universe. Wielding the enchanted hammer Mjolnir, Thor possesses incredible strength, the ability to control lightning, and near-immortality. As the prince of Asgard and a founding member of the Avengers, Thor is known for his bravery, loyalty, and sense of honor. Despite his godly powers, Thor often struggles with the responsibilities of leadership and the burden of his legacy, making him a complex and relatable character.

SPIDER-MAN
Spider-Man, or Peter Parker, is one of Marvel's most beloved and relatable heroes. After being bitten by a radioactive spider, Peter gains spider-like abilities, including super strength, agility, and the ability to cling to walls. With his iconic web-shooters, Spider-Man swings through New York City, fighting crime and protecting its citizens. Despite his extraordinary powers, Peter Parker is just a young man trying to balance the responsibilities of being a hero with the challenges of everyday life. Guided by the principle that "with great power comes great responsibility," Spider-Man is a symbol of hope and resilience, always striving to do the right thing, no matter the cost.
